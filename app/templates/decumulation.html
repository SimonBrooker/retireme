Die with Zero

What it takes to spend down every retirement account to (almost) nothing by age {{ profile.end_age }} — not leave money unused, not run out early. Each account amortizes itself independently (the same maths behind a mortgage payment, run in reverse) using its own growth rate, starting later if it's pension money locked until age {{ tax_settings.pension_access_age }}. Because it's a constant payment, the amount stays level within each "income source" period and the balance declines smoothly underneath it — it doesn't taper off gradually before the end, the same way a mortgage payment doesn't shrink as you approach payoff. Doesn't currently model contributions continuing into retirement, or the inflation-adjusted withdrawal style used elsewhere on this page.

Assumptions

Prepopulated with current UK figures — change any of these if rules change, or to model your own scenario.

Personal allowance

Tax-free income each year, for SIPP/pension/State Pension income — not ISA, which is always tax-free regardless.
Allowance reduces by £1 for every £2 of income over this.

Income tax bands

Thresholds are total income levels, not band widths — e.g. the basic rate applies to income between your allowance and the basic-rate threshold.

Applies above the higher-rate threshold.

State Pension

Pension access

UK "Normal Minimum Pension Age" — currently 55, legislated to rise to 57 from April 2028. If you retire before this age, withdrawals are modeled as coming from ISA/other accessible accounts first.

Inflation

Only used by the "inflation-adjusted" withdrawal style and "today's money" display above — doesn't affect anything else in the app.
